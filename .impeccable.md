# .impeccable.md — Design Context

> Persistent design contract for Malt Keyword Tool. Updated 2026-03-25.

---

## Design Context

### Users

French freelancers (developers, designers, writers, marketers) on Malt.fr, optimizing their profile keywords to get found by clients. Context: desktop-primary, goal-oriented — they arrive with a specific skill in mind and need a fast, honest answer: _"Is this keyword worth claiming?"_

They're not data analysts. They need the insight delivered, not the raw data dumped on them.

**Secondary insight:** occurrence counts are also a proxy for **market size by profession**. "312 React Senior freelancers on Malt" tells you the entire addressable population in that niche — not just competition, but market depth. This reframes the tool from "keyword checker" to "market intelligence."

---

### Brand Personality

**Bold · Minimal · Editorial**

Sharp and direct — tells freelancers the truth even when that truth is "this keyword is saturated." The voice is confident, not reassuring. It respects the user's intelligence.

Emotional goal: the feeling of _clarity after confusion_. The freelancer leaves knowing exactly which keywords to add and which to skip.

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Primary reference:** [every.to](https://every.to)

- Dark-first, high contrast (near-black + white)
- Editorial confidence — big type at entry points, clean reading in body areas
- No gradients, no decorative elements, no shadows for decoration
- Color is semantic only — functional, never decorative
- Generous whitespace that signals quality

**Anti-references (hard no):**

- Generic SaaS purple — Semrush, Ahrefs, Ubersuggest gradients
- Startup landing page — pastel blobs, "3 easy steps", Lottie animations
- Dashboard-heavy UI — sidebar navigation, data tables everywhere, BI tool energy
- Playful/illustrated — Duolingo, Mailchimp rounded bubbly energy

**Theme:** Dark as primary. Light mode supported. Dark is the default experience and the one that should be designed first and polished most.

**Typography:** Large, heavy weights at entry points (`font-black`, `text-7xl+`). Regular weight, comfortable line-height in data/results areas. Inter (already in use) works. No custom fonts needed — weight and size do the work.

**Color system:**

- Primary surfaces: near-black (`#0a0a0a` or `#111827`)
- Primary text: white/off-white
- Muted text: neutral-400/500
- Accent: reserved — use black/white contrast instead of indigo where possible. Indigo survives for focus rings and form inputs only.
- Competition signal scale (semantic, keep as-is):
  - Rare (<50): `#10b981` emerald
  - Niche (<500): `#84cc16` lime
  - Common (<2k): `#f59e0b` amber
  - Crowded (<10k): `#f97316` orange
  - Saturated (10k+): `#ef4444` red

### Design Principles

**1. Data is the headline.**
The occurrence count is the most important element on any result — bigger than the keyword label. Never bury the number. The user came here for the number.

**2. Honest over reassuring.**
If a keyword is saturated, say "Saturated — very high competition" in red, directly. Don't soften it. The voice is a sharp friend, not a sales page.

**3. Dark with editorial confidence.**
Dark-first like every.to. Key surfaces (hero, gates, verified page, modal) are near-black with white type. Results area can be dark or elevated-dark — never a bright white card on a dark background without reason.

**4. Contrast over color.**
Reach for weight and size before reaching for color. Use the competition signal colors for what they are — signals — not decoration. The rest of the UI is monochrome.

**5. Bold entry points, calm data areas.**
The hero, email gate, and status pages are typographically bold (`font-black`, large display sizes). Search results and charts are calm, compact, scannable — the UI gets out of the way of the data.

**6. Market intelligence framing.**
Where possible, reframe raw numbers as market facts. "312 freelancers" → "312 React Senior developers on Malt." The tool reveals the shape of a market, not just keyword difficulty.

---

### Copy Voice

- Short sentences. Direct statements. No hedging.
- Sentence fragments are fine: "Your competitive edge." "See what others don't."
- Numbers formatted French-style: `4 820` not `4,820`
- Keyword competition copy (keep existing pattern):
  - "Rare — strong opportunity"
  - "Saturated — very high competition"
- Gates and states use the punchy editorial voice already established in `/verified`

---

### Current Inconsistencies to Resolve

The codebase has a style split:

- `/verified` page: dark, editorial, `font-black`, monochrome ✓
- Hero + ResultsList + cards: light, indigo accent, conventional SaaS ✗
- EmailGate modal: white card on black backdrop — partially right

**Target state:** Align the Hero, SearchInput, KeywordCard, and ResultsList to the dark editorial direction. The verified page already shows the right energy — bring the rest of the product up to that standard.
